\begin{rSection}{Research Experience}

\begin{rSubsection}{Louisiana State University} {\em Jan 2025 - ongoing}{Postdoctoral Researcher, Advisor: Dr. Muriel Bruckner}{Baton Rouge, LA, USA}
\item Numerical modeling of the evolution of Wax Lake Delta, a part of the Mississippi River Delta system in coastal Louisiana
\item Implementing 2D hydrodynamic model ANUGA (Australian National University and Geoscience Australia) with vegetation life-cycle model to understand the feedback on sediment accretion 
\end{rSubsection}

\begin{rSubsection}{Oblique bubble screen to control the passage of aquatic invasive species}{\em Aug 2021 - July 2024}{Part of PhD dissertation, Advisor: Prof. Rafael Tinoco }{UIUC}
\item Using bubble screens to deter the movement of grass carp, an invasive fish species in Midwest region in its different life stages - eggs, larvae and young fish
\item Different configurations of the bubble screen were developed and tested in a laboratory flume
\item Detailed hydrodynamics around the bubble screen was acquired using ADV and surface PIV. The data helped us predict the movement of fish eggs for different configurations of the bubble screens. 
\end{rSubsection}

\begin{rSubsection}{Open channel flow with floating vegetation} {\em Aug 2019 - Aug 2022}{Part of PhD dissertation, Advisor: Prof. Rafael Tinoco }{UIUC}
\item Experimental study to investigate the impacts of suspended canopies on hydrodynamics and sediment transport in open channel flow
\item Different surrogates were used to study the impact of the morphology of the canopies. PIV data showed different scales of turbulence present within and underneath the suspended root-canopies.
\end{rSubsection}

\begin{rSubsection}{Optimal Rehabilitation of Gas Distribution Pipelines in Liquefiable Soils Considering Seismic Reliability}{\em Aug 2017 - Aug 2019}{Master's thesis  project, Advisor: Prof. Kalyan Piratla}{Clemson University}
\item Developed framework for optimal rehabilitation of city-level gas distribution network for regions prone to soil-liquefaction using numerical models
\item Integrated numerical gas-flow models and pipeline fragility models subject to historical seismic event to predict the disruption at the network level 
\item Genetic algorithm optimization was used in optimal design and rehabilitation planning of the network
\end{rSubsection}

\begin{rSubsection}{Data driven co-simulation models of interdependent infrastructure systems for adaptive control \href{https://nsf.gov/awardsearch/showAward?AWD_ID=1638321&HistoricalAwards=false}{[Abstract-Link]}}{\em Aug 2017 - Aug 2019}{Graduate research project, Advisor: Prof. Kalyan Piratla}{Clemson University}
\item Developed numerical models for natural gas transmission and distribution network from scratch. The model was co-simulated with power systems and water distribution network at small time steps to study the cascading effects among Power-Water-Gas infrastructures.  
\item The modeling framework is demonstrated using real-world infrastructure data from Charleston region in South Carolina, USA. 
\end{rSubsection}
